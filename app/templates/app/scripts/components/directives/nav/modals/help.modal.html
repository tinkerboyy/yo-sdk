Why is GSA launching the Acquisition Gateway?

  • The mission of GSA’s Federal Acquisition Service (FAS) is to make it easier, faster and less costly to do government acquisition.  

 

  • Within FAS, the Common Acquisition Platform (CAP) team’s Acquisition Gateway is an online workplace that will help deliver the benefits of category management and, in the future, make it easier and faster for government buyers to get through every step of the full acquisition process.

 

  • A view of all the existing contract vehicles from across government, current market trends and expertise, transactional data, and good practices will help buyers navigate the cluttered acquisition landscape and drive the government to Act as One. This will lead to improved requirements, stronger negotiations, less redundancy, and data-driven purchasing decisions.

 

What is the Acquisition Gateway?

  • The CAP Acquisition Gateway is a workplace for the federal acquisition community. Through the Acquisition Gateway, we'll empower the federal government to Act as One-- working smarter, faster, and better.  

 

  • The Gateway is one common portal that provides online access to the Category Hallways- a single location and online workspace for government procurement professionals with resources like a matrix of existing contracts from across government, data and prices paid information, market trends and analysis, and best practices.   

 

  • The Gateway will help buyers navigate the cluttered acquisition landscape, leading to improved requirements, stronger negotiations, less redundancy, and data-driven purchasing decisions.

 

  • To date, GSA has launched three category hallways, Administrative Support, IT Hardware and IT Software. More category hallways will be launched over the next nine months.

 

Why should I register as a user of the Acquisition Gateway?

 

  • GSA’s is building the Acquisition Gateway to be the hub of an acquisition professional’s daily activities.  The tools, expertise and content in the hallways comes from agencies all across government to form an agnostic, bias-free place for contracting professionals to do their work.

 

  • The Acquisition Gateway will guide federal procurement professionals through data-driven procurement actions that help agencies comply with regulations, meet socio-economic acquisition goals, and support program teams delivering on the agency mission.

 

  • The Gateway will help buyers navigate the cluttered acquisition landscape, leading to improved requirements, stronger negotiations, less redundancy, and data-driven purchasing decisions.

  • The CAP Acquisition Gateway will eventually evolve into a community of practice for procurement, we need the collective wisdom and expertise of government acquisition professionals from across every federal agency to make this tool the best it can be for everyone.

Is this tool for GSA only?

  • The Acquisition Gateway and the hallways are being developed for use by all acquisition professionals in the federal government.

 

  • Beyond the immediate acquisition community, GSA anticipates that the CIO community will be interested in the Software and Hardware hallways and purchase card holders will want to use the Administrative Support hallway for office supplies. Other communities will be interested as further hallways are launched throughout FY15.

 

  • GSA is leading the development of the platform in collaboration with the OMB Office of Federal Procurement Policy and the Strategic Sourcing Leadership Council which is made up of representatives from agencies across government. The Office of Personnel Management is building a training and education hallway and Department of Defense is building a small package delivery hallway in partnership with GSA using the Acquisition Gateway platform.

 

  • However, GSA does not intend to exert ownership or bias related to the content and expertise in the hallways.

 

  • The site’s content has been developed in partnership with category experts across the government to ensure we are collecting and providing the expertise and data that contracting professionals need, regardless of the agency in which the expertise resides.

 

How is GSA working with other agencies?  

  • Because expertise resides across the government, GSA’s partner agency relationships are critical to the success of the CAP Acquisition Gateway and hallways.

 

  • Collaboration and sharing of data, contracts and expertise between agencies is the foundation of the gateway and category hallways.  

 

  • For example, as we build and populate the next set of hallways, Small Package Delivery and Education & Training, we're working with the category managers and teams at DoD and OPM to identify contract vehicles, data, trends, content and expertise in those categories.  We are also working on a hallway for Professional Services to better serve those buyers.

 

  • Category managers and content experts from across government are curating the resources and tools available through the hallways, constantly updating the information to ensure it’s both relevant and valuable to the acquisition workforce.

 

Why is GSA developing this tool?

  • Federal procurement is complicated and challenging.  GSA’s Federal Acquisition Service is the only civilian agency in the federal government whose mission is acquisition and we want to work with all of our stakeholders to make it easier.  

 

  • We know what contracting professionals need to do their job and to make tough decisions and we have insight into the needs and priorities of the federal buyer.

 

  • We're building tools, resources, and communities that truly help acquisition professionals achieve better outcomes for the American people.  

 

Can media or industry have access to the hallways?

  • As the Gateway evolves, we expect to implement various levels of access so that we can share aspects of this resource with our other stakeholders and partners.

 

  • Over the coming year we will be looking into providing an “industry view” for the Acquisition Gateway.

  • While the hallways are a tool exclusively for government professionals, industry’s expertise, gathered through the category managers, will guide us as we develop strategies for each category, and it will help us populate these hallways with usable and valuable content and insights.

 

Which categories hallway will be launched next and when?  

  • The IT Hardware, IT Software, and Office Supplies hallways are open now and we expect to open hallways for Professional Services, Small Package Delivery, and Education & Training by the end of the calendar year.  

 

  • We have strategic plans to launch additional category hallways throughout FY2015.

 

What is Category Management?

  • Category Management groups similar goods and services as business units, or categories, in order to manage spending and leverage the government’s buying power.

 

  • Category management is all about leveraging data, best practices, subject matter expertise and existing contract vehicles, leading to smarter decisions, better purchasing options, and lower costs.  

 

How do the CAP Acquisition Gateway and Category Management work together?

  • Category Management is a fundamental transformation of the way the GSA and the government buys goods and services.

 

  • CAP’s Acquisition Gateway will help us transform our buying behaviors by pulling all of the purchasing data, buying activity, best practices and contracts available into one place.

 

  • CAP will assist acquisition professionals by providing all of the curated category expertise and data that is available governmentwide in one single tool.

When will the CAP Acquisition Gateway and the hallways be completed?
